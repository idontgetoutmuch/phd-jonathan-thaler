\begin{abstract}
When developing a model for an Agent-Based Simulation (ABS) it is of very importance to select the right update-strategy for the agents to produce the desired results. In this paper we develop a systematic treatment of all general properties, derive the possible update-strategies in ABS and discuss their interpretation and semantics something which is still lacking in the literature on ABS. Further we investigate the suitability of three very different programming languages Java, Haskell and  Scala with Actors to implement each of the update-strategies. Thus this papers contribution is the development of a new, general terminology of update-strategies and their implementation comparison in various kinds of programming languages.
\end{abstract}
